# Notes 6

1. What is Apache Pig?

A high-level data flow language built on top of MapReduce to simplify big data processing.

⸻

2. Who developed Apache Pig?

👉 Yahoo  ￼

⸻

3. Why was Pig created?

To reduce complexity and development time of MapReduce.

⸻

4. What is Pig Latin?

A data flow language used to express transformations.

⸻

5. What does Pig Latin compile into?

👉 MapReduce jobs  ￼

⸻

6. What is the biggest advantage of Pig?

👉 Less coding, faster development

⸻

7. What is the main philosophy of Pig?

👉 “Do less and accomplish more”  ￼

⸻

8. What type of language is Pig Latin?

👉 Procedural (data flow based)

⸻

9. What is a relation in Pig?

👉 A bag of tuples

⸻

10. What is a tuple?

👉 Ordered set of fields

⸻

11. What is a bag?

👉 Collection of tuples (can have duplicates)

⸻

12. What is a field?

👉 Atomic value

⸻

13. What is the default data type in Pig?

👉 bytearray  ￼

⸻

14. What are Pig execution modes?
	•	Local mode
	•	Hadoop (MapReduce) mode

⸻

15. Which is default execution mode?

👉 Hadoop mode  ￼

⸻

16. What is Local mode used for?

👉 Development and testing

⸻

17. What is Grunt?

👉 Interactive shell for Pig  ￼

⸻

18. What triggers execution in Pig?

👉 DUMP or STORE  ￼

🔥 VERY IMPORTANT

⸻

19. What is lazy evaluation in Pig?

Pig does not execute until output is required.

⸻

20. Why is lazy evaluation important?

👉 Avoids unnecessary computation → better performance  ￼

⸻

21. What is LOAD?

Loads data into a relation.

⸻

22. What is STORE?

Saves data to storage.

⸻

23. What is DUMP?

Displays output to console.

⸻

24. What is FILTER?

Removes unwanted rows.

⸻

25. What is FOREACH GENERATE?

Transforms each record.

⸻

26. What is GROUP BY?

Groups records into bags.

⸻

27. What is ORDER BY?

Sorts data.

⸻

28. What does LIMIT do?

Restricts number of records.

⸻

29. What is DISTINCT?

Removes duplicates.

⸻

30. What is JOIN?

Combines relations (flat output).
